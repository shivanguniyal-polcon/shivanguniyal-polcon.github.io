My career has progressed from studying political theory to practicing data-driven governance, and now to technical policy engineering. The timeline below outlines my professional roles, core learnings, and the certifications that supported each transition.

## 1. Policy Engineering & AI (2025–Present)
**Role:** Master of Public Policy (MPP) Candidate at IIT Delhi
*   **Focus:** Examining the intersection of democratic institutions and artificial intelligence.
*   **Key Learning:** I shifted my focus from traditional governance models to building scalable, localized AI agents. I currently research open-source models to maintain data autonomy rather than relying on closed cloud ecosystems.
*   **Anchoring Certification:** *Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals* (IBM, Jun 2025) – Formalized my technical AI baseline to merge machine learning with public policy frameworks.

## 2. Data-Driven Governance (2022–2023)
**Role:** Governance and Electioneering Consultant at Nation with NaMo
*   **Focus:** Executing electoral strategies using large-scale datasets.
*   **Key Learning:** I learned to translate broad political objectives into actionable ground-level strategies. Managing complex campaign data required moving beyond basic analysis into advanced data handling and stakeholder reporting.
*   **Anchoring Certification:** *Microsoft Excel - Data Analysis Expert* (Six Sigma Academy, Jan 2022) – Provided the analytical toolkit needed to manage large electoral datasets.

## 3. Legislative Analysis & Economics (2021–2022)
**Role:** LAMP Fellow at PRS Legislative Research
*   **Focus:** Analyzing national legislation and drafting parliamentary questions.
*   **Key Learning:** Working closely with a Member of Parliament showed me how laws are constructed and debated. I realized that effective policy analysis requires both economic literacy and programming logic to process legislative data efficiently.
*   **Anchoring Certifications:**
    *   *Globalization, Economic Growth and Stability* (IE Business School, Aug 2020) – Solidified my understanding of macroeconomic forces affecting policy.
    *   *Programming for Everybody (Python)* (University of Michigan, Jun 2020) – My first technical step, which laid the groundwork for my later transition into policy engineering.

## 4. Foundational Research & Theory (2017–2021)
**Role:** B.A. (Hons.) in Governance & Public Administration at PDEU (Gold Medalist) & Research Intern
*   **Focus:** Ground-level research on urban sanitation, migration, and political ethics.
*   **Key Learning:** Fieldwork with municipal corporations and state governments taught me the practical realities of policy implementation and the human impact of governance failures, while my coursework provided the theoretical foundation.
*   **Anchoring Certifications:**
    *   *Moral Foundations of Politics* (Yale University, May 2020) – Grounded my research in ethical political theory.
    *   *Probability and Statistics Master* (Krista King Math, Apr 2020) – Ensured my early research was backed by sound statistical reasoning.
